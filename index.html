Decriminalizing Prostitution

By Tyrell Khan

Home Kidney Sales Weed Trade

From an ethical egoist perspective, individuals should be free to pursue actions that maximize their self-interest, provided they do not violate the rights of others. Prostitution, when conducted voluntarily between consenting adults, represents a mutual exchange of value.

Criminalization pushes the industry underground, increasing risk and reducing safety. Decriminalization would allow individuals to act rationally in their own interest, improve working conditions, and reduce exploitation.

Kidney Sales | Ethical Egoism

Legalizing Kidney Sales

By Tyrell Khan

Home Prostitution Weed Trade

Ethical egoism holds that individuals own their bodies. Preventing kidney sales restricts autonomy. If a person rationally decides selling a kidney benefits them, they should be allowed to do so.

A regulated market could reduce illegal trade and increase organ availability while allowing individuals to pursue self-interest through voluntary exchange.

Weed Trade | Ethical Egoism

Legalizing the Sale of Weed

By Tyrell Khan

Home Prostitution Kidney Sales

From an ethical egoist perspective, banning weed sales restricts voluntary exchange. Individuals should be free to produce, sell, and consume cannabis if done consensually.

Legalization would reduce crime, empower entrepreneurs, and align with the principle of rational self-interest.
